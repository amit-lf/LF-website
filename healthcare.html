The Challenge

Healthcare procurement contracts carry risks that generic review misses.

Regulatory exposure, supply dependency, and liability imbalance are built into the standard terms vendors send. By the time they surface, you've already signed.

dConstructAI reviews from the buyer's perspective — hospital, health system, or procurement team — and flags what matters before you commit.

What You Walk Away Knowing

These are the questions dConstructAI answers — so your decision is informed, not assumed.

Is your supplier permitted to suspend deliveries unilaterally — and if so, under what conditions and with how much notice?

Does this agreement involve patient data sharing that requires a Business Associate Agreement before it's signed?

Is this an exclusive sourcing arrangement — and what does that cost you in alternatives, leverage, and pricing flexibility?

Who bears the cost of a product recall — and does that answer change depending on whether the recall was defect-triggered or regulatory-triggered?

Can the vendor increase prices without a cap over the life of the contract — and is there retroactive pricing language buried in the terms?

Does the payment or referral structure create any exposure under federal anti-kickback rules?

If the vendor claims force majeure, who decides whether alternate sourcing was genuinely unavailable — and is that decision theirs alone?

Are there FDA approval or clearance dependencies that create compliance gaps if the vendor's status changes after signing?
