The Challenge

IP and licensing terms in technology contracts are where the real risk lives.

Ownership ambiguity, retained vendor rights, and licensing gaps are written into standard agreements in language that sounds reasonable until it isn't.

dConstructAI reviews from the customer's perspective — with role detection — and surfaces what you actually own versus what you think you own.

What You Walk Away Knowing

These are the questions dConstructAI answers — so your decision is informed, not assumed.

When the engagement ends, which party owns the designs, layouts, and test data — and is that written clearly enough to be enforceable?

Does the license allow sublicensing to your foundry or test house for manufacturing — or does it block the relationship you need?

Is ownership language precise enough to be enforceable — or does "related to" or "arising from" leave room for a dispute you didn't anticipate?

If IP is jointly owned, can either party license it to your competitors without your knowledge or consent?

What happens to your design files, yield data, and characterization results when the vendor relationship ends?

Does the vendor retain rights to reuse methodology or know-how developed specifically for your project — in their next engagement with a competitor?

Are improvements and derivatives explicitly assigned to you — or do broad vendor grant clauses give them rights over work you paid for?
